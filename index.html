Documenting the Evolution of the UNESCO World Heritage Program

Established in 1972, UNESCO's World Heritage Site Program seeks to protect sites of cultural, historical, scientific, or other forms of significance that are deemed to be of outstanding universal value. Currently, there are 1,121 World Heritage sites within 167 countries, with cultural sites and western European countries dominating the list. This project tracks how this program has evolved over time, particularly regarding how the application of the program has changed.
